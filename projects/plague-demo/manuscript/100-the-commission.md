Matthaeus accepted because refusal was impossible and because explanation, in itself, was a duty. He set his astrolabe beside the almanac and asked Etienne to prepare two ledgers: one for accepted causes, one for observations not yet reconciled. He believed reconciliation was always possible if one respected hierarchy: Providence, stars, elements, humors, symptoms.

When Raoul asked whether the conjunction alone could bear the whole argument, Matthaeus answered that celestial influence was primary but not solitary. A universal disposition still required local instruments. The canon said instruments were admissible so long as they did not multiply panic.

After the canon left, Matthaeus unlocked a coffer beneath the desk and withdrew a loose quire copied from a prohibited tradition of astral image-magic. He consulted it only in private, not for doctrine but for method: how to reason from correspondences when causes were hidden. Etienne saw the motion and looked away. Between them, this silence had long been a professional courtesy.
